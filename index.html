🐾 The Pup Café 🐾

A café for dogs, workers & mutual care
Created by Hunter Harris & Stephen Barksdale — founders, workers, lovers of dogs
ThePupCafeIdea@Gmail.com | @ThePupCafeIdea

The Problem

We live in a system that undervalues care: for animals, for workers, and for each other. Loneliness is rising. Dogs are abandoned. Café workers live on poverty wages.

The Pup Café Solution

Not a novelty, not a stunt — a full-system alternative built around rescue, dignity, and worker care.

🐾 Immediate Impact

  • Lessens pressure on kill shelters — finds homes, matches dogs to roles (comfort, adoption, ESA), and builds care around their needs
  • Real worker careers — pay grows with the café, not at its expense; worker input shapes everything from menus to policies
  • Daily enrichment + rest — dogs aren’t décor; they get calm, stimulation, and real attention
  • Living-wage jobs from day one — unless the business is in crisis, no poverty wages, ever

🌟 Short-Term Goals

  • Monthly adoption events — rotating partner orgs, foster networks, and municipal shelters in crisis
  • Popups with local vendors, healers, artists — tables for those shut out of traditional markets
  • Mutual aid fridge — stocked with excess from the café, dog food donations, and local offerings
  • Community gathering turns into action — people shape what happens here, not just consume it

🌱 Long-Term Vision

  • Flexible profit-sharing instead of co-op ownership — but with open idea channels and worker priority
  • Outreach network — find dogs, train them, place them; build care into logistics, not just slogans
  • Dedicated transitional dog housing — for dogs not yet ready for café life or immediate placement
  • Certified service/ESA dog training — lower-cost access for those often priced out of care
  • Training track for dog-handling careers — especially for people left out of traditional roles
  • Toolkit to replicate — open-source model so others can build their own care-centered café
  • Nonprofit arm — focused on education, storytelling, and funding rescue systems

🌈 Possibilities Are Open

  • Grab a coffee — drop your dog off for the day (with enrichment & supervision)
  • Launch nonprofit-style education — dog behavior, rescue, community care
  • Offer paid certification tracks — training, caregiving, volunteer prep
  • Pilot what's needed — short-term fostering, grief support, food pantry
  • Keep experimenting — if it serves dogs, workers, or the neighborhood, it's worth trying
